Consider Dog Daycare for Your Pup

While some dogs enjoy being home alone, others may require more excitement and activity. If your pup fits into the latter category, then dog daycare may be the perfect fit! PupHub, a nationally recognized all-inclusive dog care facility, offers the best dog daycare near you.

Our core philosophy focuses on appropriate playtime and exercise for every pup in carefully selected playgroups. Pups who participate in daycare at PupHub are more adjusted, less stressed, healthier, and happier.

Benefits of Dog Daycare

There are a couple of added benefits when considering dog daycare for your furry family member.

Recreation

Your pet will be less likely to be destructive out of boredom. Pups that are left home alone can express their separation anxiety through destructive behavior like chewing and clawing up your belongings.

Exercise

Dog daycare is an outlet for their energy. Regular mental and physical exercise is critical for your dog’s health, and at daycare, they’ll have a focused outlet for their excess energy.

Getaway

Your dog will thank you for the change of scenery. It doesn’t hurt to switch up your dog’s routine. Dog daycare will be a mini-dogcation for your pet!

Daycare Prerequisites

The safety and health of your pet are our first concern. All dogs that participate in daycare must demonstrate adequate social skills and the ability to play safely. Additionally, all dogs over the age of seven months must be spayed or neutered.

If we find your dog prefers people over dogs, they will instead receive personal playtime with our canine handlers.

Before overnight boarding and/or dog daycare services, you must provide proof of the following up-to-date vaccinations from your vet:

  • Bordetella

    The Bordetella vaccine is a non-core vaccine that is administered to both dogs and cats that frequent daycare or boarding facilities to decrease likelihood of developing canine tracheobronchitis, commonly known as kennel cough.

  • Parvovirus & Distemper

    The two most commonly diagnosed systemic viral conditions in dogs.

  • Rabies

    The rabies vaccine is classed as an immunizing agent, which involves injecting a deactivated strain of the virus into the pet, which in turn prompts the dog’s immune system to produce antibodies to the virus, thereby protecting them against later contracting an active strain of the disease.

Please note, that we will accept titer tests for Distemper and Rabies. We also require that your dog be on a regular flea and tick prevention program, such as Frontline or Advantage. (Flea Powders/Collars are not acceptable, with the exception of the Seresto Collar.)

Dogs over 7 months must be spayed or neutered in order to participate in daycare, however they may still be boarded.
